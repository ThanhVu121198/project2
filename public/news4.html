Learn about handicraft products at Vietnam House

Vietnam house, designed by architect Vo Trong Nghia with the main materials of wood and bamboo, has just opened to visitors at 187 Nguyen Van Huong, Thao Dien Ward, District 2, Ho Chi Minh City. Ho Chi Minh City.

With an area of 2,300 m2 and located next to the Saigon River, Vietnam House consists of 50 stalls displaying handicraft products that are guaranteed by the investor - Thanh Dat Company - as "100 hand-made Vietnamese goods. %‚Äù and 12 miniature production models representing 12 groups of handicraft villages in Vietnam, including: bamboo and rattan, products from sedge and water hyacinth, ceramics, wood carving, lacquer, embroidery, stone carving, handmade weaving, handmade paper, art paintings, metal and other handicraft products.

Visitors can not only see the products but also see the artisans making the products, and can try them by themselves under the guidance of the artisans. In addition, there is a program of ethnic music performances from 10:30 a.m. to 11:30 a.m. every Saturday and Sunday, and there will be monthly workshops on handicraft production.

June, September 28, 2017 - By: Quang Anh.
